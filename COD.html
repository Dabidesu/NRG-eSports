Dylan ENVOY Hannon

Twitter: twitter.com/DylanEnvoy
Twitch: twitch.tv/DylanEnvoy

Envoy quickly established himself as a top Black Ops 4 player after winning two big CMG tournaments with pick up teams. He joined up with Charullz, GodFormz, Performal, and Remedy in the beginning of November. Midnight Esports signed the squad going into the CWL Las Vegas United States Qualifier. They won the qualifier and received travel and accommodation to the event. The team placed Top 40 at Vegas. Midnight revamped their roster before the end of the month. Keeping only Envoy, they added LlamaGod, Parzelion, Brack, and JetLi to the roster before the Pro League Qualifier. At the qualifier, the team went a perfect 6-0 in their pool, defeating teams such as 100 Thieves, Pittsburgh Knights, and Team Space along the way to automatic qualification to the CWL Pro League 2019.

Troy SENDER Michaels

Twitter: twitter.com/Sender_FN
Twitch: twitch.tv/Sender
YouTube: youtube.com/MLGSender

Sender started playing the Call of Duty series back in 2008 on Call of Duty 4, and started looking into competitive gaming in Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2 when pub stomping kills became to easy for him. Sender first started out playing Search & Destroy only on GameBattles Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2 and Black Ops team ladders. With the release of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3, Sender started playing MLG variant and started attending local LAN's in the New Jersey area where he had to compete with players such as MBoZe, Saint, Methodz, Fluff, and many more high profile opponents. Sender started to become well known while apart of TEC & Clock Work. In Call of Duty: Ghosts, Sender helped lead aDversity to a top 10 finish at MLG Fall Championship.
